Every person has a personal philosophy, even if they cannot clearly define it. This includes internalized values, beliefs, assumptions, and judgments about:

  • Right and wrong (Ethics)
  • Reality and existence (Metaphysics)
  • Truth and knowledge (Epistemology)

These presuppositions shape decisions, behavior, and conscience. They are often unconscious but guide everyday life.

Philosophy also means the activity of examining and analyzing our beliefs. This involves questioning:

  • Why we believe what we believe
  • The foundation of our moral values
  • The basis of knowledge and truth

Professional philosophers like Plato, Aristotle, Kant, and others engaged in systematic reflection. This level goes beyond ordinary thinking and seeks clarity and logical structure.
