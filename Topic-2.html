The AS-AD Framework

1. What is the AS-AD Model?

The Aggregate Supply–Aggregate Demand (AS-AD) model explains how total output (Y) and the price level (P) are determined in the entire economy.

It combines demand-side forces (AD) and supply-side forces (AS) to determine macroeconomic equilibrium.

2. Aggregate Demand (AD)

AD represents total spending in the economy.

  • AD = C + I + G + (X - M)
  • Downward sloping due to price level effects

3. Aggregate Supply (AS)

AS shows the relationship between total output and price level.

Short Run AS (SRAS)

  • Upward sloping
  • Unemployment present
  • Output below full capacity (Y < Yf)

Long Run AS (LRAS)

  • Vertical at full employment output (Y = Yf)
  • Economy operating at capacity

Very Long Run

  • All inputs variable
  • Capital accumulation increases Yf

4. Why AS Has Different Slopes

  • Short run: Sticky wages and excess capacity
  • Long run: Resource constraints bind
  • Very long run: Capital and technology adjust

5. Short-Run vs Long-Run Adjustment

If output deviates from Yf, price adjustments occur over time.

When Y > Yf → inflationary pressure. When Y < Yf → recessionary pressure.

6. Shifts in AD

  • Changes in consumption
  • Investment shifts
  • Fiscal policy
  • Monetary policy
  • Net exports

7. Shifts in AS

  • Technology changes
  • Input prices
  • Productivity
  • Capital stock

8. Long-Run Determinants

Over long periods, output is determined by:

  • Capital
  • Labor
  • Technology
  • Institutions

9. Policy Interpretation

  • Demand shocks affect output in short run
  • Supply shocks affect inflation and output
  • Long run determined by productive capacity
