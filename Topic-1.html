Introduction to Macroeconomics

1. What is Macroeconomics?

Macroeconomics studies the economy as a whole. It focuses on aggregate outcomes such as total output, total income, employment, inflation, and economic growth.

Key Focus: National income, unemployment, inflation, economic growth, business cycles.

2. Macroeconomics vs Microeconomics

  • Microeconomics: Individual consumers and firms.
  • Macroeconomics: Entire economy and aggregate variables.

3. Major Macroeconomic Goals

  • Economic Growth
  • Full Employment
  • Price Stability
  • External Balance
A healthy economy aims for sustained growth, low unemployment, and stable prices.

4. Gross Domestic Product (GDP)

GDP measures the total market value of all final goods and services produced within a country in a given period.

  • Expenditure Approach: C + I + G + (X - M)
  • Income Approach
  • Production Approach

5. Business Cycles

Economies fluctuate over time through:

  • Expansion
  • Peak
  • Recession
  • Trough

6. Inflation

Inflation is the sustained increase in the general price level. It reduces purchasing power.

7. Unemployment

  • Frictional
  • Structural
  • Cyclical

8. Policy Tools

  • Fiscal Policy: Government spending and taxation.
  • Monetary Policy: Central bank control of money supply and interest rates.
Macroeconomics studies how policy influences national income, inflation, and employment.
